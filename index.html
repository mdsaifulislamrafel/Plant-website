on our environment

Plants absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen through photosynthesis, which improves air quality and helps slow climate change.

Quick List of Our Features

Plants are the eukaryotes that form the kingdom Plantae; they are predominantly photosynthetic. This means that they obtain their energy from sunlight, using chloroplasts derived from endosymbiosis with cyanobacteria to produce sugars from carbon dioxide and water, using the green pigment chlorophyll.

Features That Might Attract You

Reduces Greenhouse Gas

Carbon dioxide the ‘greenhouse gas’ is notorious for its heat-trapping properties.

Reduce Soil Erosion

Planting trees has sustainable benefits to the environment too. Rain and wind are the main forces that damage bare soil causing erosion.

Prevent Water Pollution

With many substances in the atmosphere, stormwater picks up many pollutants, dirt, and bacteria that cause a threat to clean water in many places.

Save Water

Planting trees helps to save water. As trees provide shade, the water from low vegetation will evaporate slowly. Newly planted trees only require 15 gallons of water in a week.
